Visual Retro

Xerox | UI Designer | 2000 - 2006

In the early days of my design journey, I had the opportunity to delve into the world of creating icons, graphics, and animations for user interfaces. These artifacts, though visually distinct from today's design standards, hold a special place in my portfolio as they represent the starting point of my creative exploration.

While the aesthetics may appear dated through a contemporary lens, these early works showcase my ability to adapt and thrive within the technical confines of the era. They serve as a testament to my resourcefulness and creative problem-solving skills, as well as my willingness to embrace new challenges.

Revisiting these retro graphics not only sparks a sense of nostalgia but also highlights the remarkable evolution of design and technology over the years. They stand as a reminder of the ever-changing nature of our industry and the importance of continuous learning and growth.

As you explore this section of my portfolio, I invite you to appreciate these early endeavors not merely for their visual appeal but for the journey they represent — a journey that has shaped my design sensibilities and paved the way for the more sophisticated and polished work that followed.

16-color icons

Back in the day, windows apps safely supported only 16-colors. (Not 16-bit but 16 specific colors) When there were multiple versions of windows around, we had to design to the lowest common denominator (windows 3.1) and stick with the 16-colors so code did not need to be recompiled and re-tested.
