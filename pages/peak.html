Peak Productivity

EagleView | Research and Design | 2018-2019

EagleView creates mapping and measurement products from high-resolution aerial photos. However, they need human beings to review many thousands of images to check for focus, cloud-cover, and other anomalies. Their current system was labor intensive and caused burnout and employee pain. I was hired to fix that.

Legacy Design

Existing software was laborious and clunky.

  • Controls were a series of small, unlabeled icons
  • Images wwwere displayed in the order of the computer byte blocks
  • Adjacent images were rarely next to each other.

New Design

The new design vastly accelerated the most common tasks and automated others. Selecting images and adding tags is faster and more standardized.

RESEARCH: Understanding the User needs

I shadowed and interviewed several current Geomatics QC technicians to understand their job and current workflow. I learned a lot about their work and identified several ways a new design could speed their process. I showed them early versions of the design for their input as well.

Technicians did needed different information at different stages of their work.

  • They only needed the file name if selecting an image. Otherwise it takes up valuable space
  • Likewise, metadata is relevant only in certain diagnostic situations.
  • Less than 1% of images are viewed enlarged but any image may be chosen.
  • Images with anomalies may need further review but they were difficult to locate when mixed within the full image set.
  • Technicians very quickly visually scanned images for anomalies.
  • They needed not only to reject these images but to mark the reason for the rejection.
  • Marking images with problems such as cloud-cover, focus, or oil-on-lens would involve selecting multiple images one at a time and required several clicks to reject and note the reason.
  • Finding images with problems such as cloud-cover, focus, or oil-on-lens would involve selecting multiple images one at a time and required several clicks to reject and note the reason.
  • Images were previously in a random order based on memory blocks. This made it inefficient to select adjacent images with the same cloud-cover for instance.

Innovations

  • I determined that if they’re arranged by each of 6 cameras and time stamp, the images would now be in a spatially adjacent grid making it easy to select groups.
  • Sometimes a camera problem would eliminate a series of images. With images randomly placed, manual selection was difficult. Now the entire camera (column) could be selected easily for comment or rejection.
  • Time stamp also allowed selection across rows with similar advantages.
  • Selecting the “line” selects a large group related to the motion of the aerial cameras, also useful for certain anomalies.
  • Tags are created by the QC community. When user types a tag, it becomes part of the autocomplete menu.
  • Once tagged, images would also be sorted into tabs for later review.

Selecting columns, rows, and lines

Users can easily select single images, rows, columns or lines (common tasks in image QC).

The homepage gives a visual overview of all the active surveys ready for QC. Color and tooltips would denote the current status of the survey. Tabs helped to sort but user and current status.

Individual images can be enlarged providing additional data useful to understanding image coverage.

Code help

Sometimes I needed to devise solutions to developer problems so the design would get implemented as designed. I did this as a way to help expand the teams capacity.

Styles
