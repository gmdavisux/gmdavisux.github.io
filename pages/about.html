About me

I'm a Rochester, NY based designer who loves to use design to make people's work lives easier and more fulfilling.

I've always found my interests at the intersection of technology and art, long before I became an Industrial Designer â€” even as a kid, I filled notebooks with drawings of the future. As digital experiences emerged, I found my human-centered design skills seamlessly translated into the UX realm.

The most rewarding part of my work is solving real human problems through innovative yet simple design solutions. I find deep satisfaction in observing and deeply understanding people's needs, then applying an iterative process to create innovative solutions that remove frustrations and enrich people's working lives. Stripping away complexities to deliver purposeful tools that make work more effortless is what drives my passion.

Outside of work, I prioritize quality time with family and friends - the core relationships that ground me. My passion for creating doesn't stop at work, however. I find fulfillment in hands-on making through carpentry, 3D printing, CNC milling, and other outlets for bringing ideas into physical reality. Personal projects allow me to hone my skills in an explorative, low-stakes environment. This mindset of craftsmanship provides wonderful balance to digital design work.

Innovation

Innovation unlocks new possibilities. I explore ways to leverage tech solutions to eliminate unnecessary work or transform existing processes.

Research

I use interviews, testing, and observation to truly understand people and their problems to provide a delightful, natural experience.

Simplicity

Simplicity, without innovation to enable it, may make a design look nice but perform poorly. I focus on innovation to enable simplicity.

Design

Good design creates intuitive and appealing experiences. I leverage design principles to effectively communicate, convey meaning, and guide desired behaviors.
