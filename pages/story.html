Story Telling

CloudCheckr/NetApp | Lead Design | 2021

CloudCheckr’s multiple best practice checks are a valuable differentiator. However, their use and management were painful, especially for large customers.

New design

Our new design focused on providing a way to tell a story with the data, allowing users to quickly understand the severity of the issues and act on them at scale.

Research

User interviews revealed:

  • Customers hated the long list they needed to carefully check for fear of missing something.
  • They also had no idea if the problems were getting worse of how long issues had persisted.
  • Finally, they needed a way to act on problems at scale, not one at a time.

Sketches

Some early concept sketches of the new design. You can see how the tab graphics later evolved into a simpler design.

Legacy Design

The old design had hundreds of individual checks, each with individual controls that required users to manipulate each one at a time. The list was so large that the user needed to scroll to see the extent and severity of the problems.

Telling a Story with Design

The new design offers several enhancements providing progressive disclosure and manipulation at scale:

  • Filters allow the inclusion of vendors or user-defined tags to further focus the data.
  • Small bars above each tab allow a preview of the severity of issues in each category.
  • A historical trend chart shows a visual history of the selected issues.
  • Each severity level is collapsed into a summary view with a graphic and numeric overview.
  • A control bar allows manipulation of multiple items at scale.
