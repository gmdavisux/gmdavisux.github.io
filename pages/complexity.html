Managing Complexity

Dell Technologies | Design Lead, study planning | 2012

The Dell K3000 Mobile Device Management Appliance was a groundbreaking server-appliance designed to streamline the management of mobile devices and their software, such as phones and tablets, across an organization.

As the lead designer for this project, I was responsible for overseeing the workflow and design decisions, collaborating closely with a cross-functional team comprising a graphic designer, user researcher, project manager, and developers.

Comprehensive Device Management

This powerful appliance empowered administrators with a comprehensive suite of capabilities to monitor and control their fleet of mobile devices effectively. From pushing software updates and security patches to enforcing corporate policies and locating misplaced or stolen devices, the K3000 offered a centralized command center for managing the entire mobile device lifecycle.

Simple but powerful search

One of the standout features of the K3000 was its intuitive search functionality. Designed to be flexible and user-friendly, the search allowed administrators to perform freeform typing and instantly presented categorized matches. This enabled them to build customized lists by either requiring (is) or excluding (is not) specific items, streamlining the process of locating and managing devices or applications.

Application Management at Scale

The appliance featured a dedicated screen that provided administrators with a comprehensive overview of all applications installed across their fleet of devices, spanning both iOS and Android platforms. From this centralized hub, they could seamlessly push updates, manage settings, and maintain consistent application deployment across their entire mobile device ecosystem.

Static Mockups: Visualizing the Concepts

In the early stages of the project, static mockups played a crucial role in communicating and refining the design concepts. These initial layout drawings served as a foundation for discussions, iterations, and collaborative decision-making, ensuring that the final product met the needs of both administrators and end-users.

The Dell K3000 Mobile Device Management Appliance exemplified our commitment to simplifying complex tasks and empowering organizations to maintain control over their mobile device ecosystem. Through its intuitive design and powerful features, the K3000 paved the way for streamlined device management, enhanced security, and improved operational efficiency.

Customer Study

During development some customers were offered an early preview release to participate in our study. The objectives of this study were to review a subset of release 1.1 features with customers and have them perform tasks using specific functionalities, including Smart labels, User roles, SSL certificates, Updates, and Volume purchase. The aim was to assess whether customers could find and use these features effectively, as well as gather their suggestions for improvement and identify any missing elements.

The methodology involved seven participants, all current K3000 owners, in a study conducted from June 26-28, 2013. Each participant engaged in a one-hour session, which was recorded. The study utilized a hybrid approach, combining a usability study where participants first attempted tasks, followed by an open conversation about the features. This method allowed for both practical assessment and in-depth discussion of the new release features.

Highlights

  • In general, participants liked the new improvements. They especially liked the ability to delete profiles
  • Creating labels was also popular with those who had a need for it
  • In many areas of the UI, it was difficult to read text because it was a light grey on a grey. Also, active options looked greyed out and not clickable. As a response, we increased the contrast in specific regions.
  • While creating user roles was ranked high as a new feature, several participants wanted more granularity to the access controls associated with user roles
  • The added SSL certificate features were very popular, especially being able to generate a new certificate right from the K3000
  • Volume purchase was appreciated by those who said they might use it
