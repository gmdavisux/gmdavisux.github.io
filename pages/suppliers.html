Replenishing Suppliers

Coupa | Lead Design, Planning | 2023

Coupa provides business spend management solutions that manage transactions between buyers and sellers.

As the principal and only designer, I collaborated with the product manager and development team to revamp the supplier side of this B2B purchasing software. Leveraging a pre-existing design system and strategy, we addressed a neglected user experience to boost engagement on a frequently skipped login page.

New design — work to be done!

From our discussions with suppliers, we learned most users skipped the old landing page due to its lack of actionable content. I led the redesign to create an intuitive hub:

  • Introduced a recent activity table to display orders and announcements.
  • Streamlined announcements for clarity and user action.
  • Enhanced supplier visibility in search and profile completion.
  • Improved navigation aligns the styles of the supplier portal to the rest of Coupa's suite.
  • Aligned with Coupas' design system for consistency. This overhaul aimed to increase user engagement, a key goal since users typically bypassed this page post-login.

Research

A dedicated UX researcher conducted extensive interviews, revealing

  • Suppliers of all sizes rarely spent time on the legacy page due to no clear next steps.
  • Content rarely changed, reducing relevance.
  • Design allowed only one customer's facts and invoices at a time, limiting efficiency. My world view analysis proposed consolidating supplier accounts into one list, improving task access.

World View

  • Research showed suppliers strongly preferred having all their accounts in one list. I proposed displaying information from multiple customer accounts.
  • Differing regional data privacy laws presented a challenge, as data needs to be stored in the country of origin.
  • Although there was initial resistance due to the difficulty, research helped convince the team this was a problem worth solving.

Legacy Design

The legacy landing page for suppliers offered minimal actionable content, prompting immediate exits as users sought real work. It was poorly designed with:

  • Competing visual elements causing confusion.
  • Stale data that rarely updated.
  • Empty profile info users never completed.

Responsive Design

Responsive design was a key consideration. The new design is responsive and works well on all devices.
