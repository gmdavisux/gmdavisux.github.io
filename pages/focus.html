Enhanced Focus

Eastman Kodak | Lead Design, Research Planning, Shared Facilitation | 2010

How can you update a successful yet outdated product and enhance the perception of its quality? In the case of the Kodak Picture Maker Kiosk, I implemented changes that centered around the user’s own images, rather than the user interface.

New design

The redesign aimed to minimize visual clutter and prioritize user-generated content.

Additional Screens

Legacy Design

The original design appeared dated and excessively ornate.

Iterative Research

Through iterative designs and user studies, we achieved a significant improvement in the perceived quality, surpassing a 20% increase compared to the previous design.

Wireframe explorations

To facilitate early design explorations and discussions, simplified wireframes were used.
