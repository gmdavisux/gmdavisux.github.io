Visualizing Sunlight

Eagleview | Lead Design | 2019

I designed this entire report but the Total Solar Resource Fraction views of the roof were a particularly interesting visual communication problem.

Eagleview wanted to create a product solar installers could use to determine the best year-round placement of solar panels. Showing this visually seemed straightforward, but presented unexpected challenges.

New design

The new report was designed to show solar contractors and their customers the best placement of solar panels for maximum efficiency year-round. The initial version was a printed document with the web version to follow.

Research

In order to get the visualizations right, I tested several different color schemes by asking participants to tell me where the sunniest part of the roof was.

Below are some early concept sketches of the new design.

Multi-color

It would seem at first that using discrete colors to represent different ranges of solar irradiance would be best. However, testing showed almost half of the participants were unable to determine which color was the brightest.

Adjacent warm colors

Continuing the experiment, I tried a transition between adjacent colors.

The problem is some people reversed which shade is “hottest” despite the fact that this appeared with a labeled key. I intended the lightest color to be the sunniest.

Monochromatic

A breakthrough happened when I began to think of areas of darkness rather than sunlight. The result was a more monochromatic heat map.

I used blue because it can appear both light and dark, clearly conveying shadow and light. This communicates the best and user testing showed this. I also tried other monochromatic colors but blues worked the best.

Gridlock

Next we added a square-foot grid to the roof to aid in measurement. Unfortunately, the lines made the blue look darker and drab.

Dot to dot

Taking inspiration from my sketchbook grid, I changed the line-grid to a dot-grid where the intersection of lines formed a dot. The dots had less of an effect on the color perceptions. Now the colors were coming through cleaner.
