Adaptive Design

Xerox | Strategy Team Member | 2004

As part of the strategy team that created the style guide, one task was to help determine how to adapt the UI strategic design to several different embedded display sizes.

A main goal of the strategic design group was coherence across products. The same design had to be scaled to fit anything from the largest full-screen displays to the smallest quarter-panel devices. This had to be done while maintaining the a usable touch-target size and readable font size. This allows a coherent user-experience across all levels of devices.

Graceful Adaptation

Shown are large 4:3 aspect ratio display (both 10-inch and 8-inch), 7-inch 16:9 aspect ratio displays, half-panel, and quarter-panel touch displays. All displays met the requirements for touch-target size and font readability. The UI architecture is similar to allow the transfer of learning and a common user experience.

At that time, most of our displays (embedded or client) supported 12-bit (2096 colors) or more. The strategic UI design elements were designed for full-color.

16-grays

Before the transition to the full-color screens Xerox employs today, most displays utilized only 16-gray levels. Through the skillful use of dithering and other techniques, I was able to adapt the strategic design elements to this display technology.

4-grays

Some of the earlier touch screens used only black, white, and two shades of gray. I was able to gracefully degrade strategic design elements to these parameters.
