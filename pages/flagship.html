Pocket-sizing a Flagship

Dell Technologies | Design Lead | 2013

The Dell K1000 Management Appliance is a workhorse IT server which allows admins to track, manage and update their entire fleet of devices from a web UI. K1000 Go is the pocket interface to this powerful appliance. 

At launch, the mobile app was adopted by almost 30% of existing desktop users.

I was the lead designer for this project, responsible for workflow and design decisions, working with another designer, a user researcher, project manager and developers.

We decided to launch with both iOS and Android and to support both tablet and phone-sized UI. We couldn’t simply scale up the design but needed to adapt the UI to take advantage of the differences while retaining a branded style.

One challenge was in refining a huge set of functions down to the most useful and most appropriate for mobile use. 

User Research & Prioritization

I collaborated with our research team to design a focused study targeting field technicians and IT managers during live client deployments. We asked a single, powerful question: “When you’re on-site, what do you *actually* need from your phone?” The answer was unanimous: no one expected to create or modify configurations on mobile—that belonged on the desktop. The real need? Instant access to the help queue to check status, triage issues, and respond in real time. With these insights in hand, I led a ruthless prioritization: we eliminated 80% of the desktop feature set, distilling the mobile app into a lightweight, mission-critical tool. This research-driven constraint transformed complexity into clarity—delivering exactly what users needed, when they needed it.
